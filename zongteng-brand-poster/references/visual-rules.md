# Visual Rules

Use this reference for Zongteng Group VI rules.

## Colors

Primary brand colors:

- Zongteng Blue: `#051D49`, RGB `5 29 73`, CMYK `C100 M99 Y59 K30`, Pantone `2768C`.
- Zongteng Orange: `#EF7622`, RGB `239 118 34`, CMYK `C0 M67 Y90 K0`, Pantone `158C`.
- White: `#FFFFFF`, RGB `255 255 255`.

Use Zongteng Blue as the dominant formal corporate color. Use Zongteng Orange as an accent for motion, emphasis, CTA, date, number, or small graphic details.

For formal posters, avoid replacing the palette with unrelated colors. Secondary colors may be used only to support diagrams, campaign mood, or value IP scenes.

## Typography

Official VI font:

- Chinese: 思源黑体 / Source Han Sans.
- English: Source Han Sans Latin or a clean geometric sans fallback.

Use bold weights for headlines, medium weights for labels, and regular weights for body text. Keep typography clean and infrastructure-grade. Avoid ornate scripts, overly cute type, or thin low-contrast body text in corporate posters.

## Logo Usage

Default logo:

- Use the horizontal Zongteng Group lockup for most posters.
- Use the vertical lockup when the layout is narrow or centered.
- Use icon-only only for tiny, simplified, or special device-like contexts.

Rules:

- Keep clear space around the logo.
- Do not place text, images, or decorative elements in the protected area.
- Keep the logo sharp and legible.
- Prefer full-color logo on white or light backgrounds.
- Use reverse/white or single-color variants only for dark backgrounds or constrained production contexts.

Minimum print sizes from VI examples:

- Horizontal logo: about `40 x 12.2mm` or `40 x 12.4mm`.
- Vertical logo: about `20 x 18.7mm`.

## Do Not

- Do not distort logo proportions.
- Do not change the logo structure.
- Do not change or add logo colors.
- Do not add strokes, glows, or decorative elements to the logo.
- Do not deform, rotate, crop, or freely reinterpret logo elements.
- Do not let a generative model redraw the logo.

## Auxiliary Graphics

The VI manual includes directional arrows, corner marks, plus-like marks, dense patterns, and motion-line fields on Zongteng Blue.

Use them as:

- Background patterns.
- Corner anchors.
- Directional dividers.
- Motion or logistics route cues.
- Low-opacity texture behind titles or data.

Keep auxiliary graphics secondary to the message and logo.

## Poster Quality

Read `design-quality-bar.md` before creating final poster files.

The poster must look designed, not like a PowerPoint page. Use a dominant hero visual, campaign composition, texture, motion, layering, or mascot/IP scene. Supporting cards and detail blocks are allowed only as secondary information areas.

Avoid:

- Centered title plus equal cards.
- Pale grid background plus generic circles as the main visual.
- Dashboard-like information blocks unless the user explicitly asked for a report poster.
- Slide-like spacing where all content sits in neat rectangles with no visual tension.
